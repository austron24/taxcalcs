About Roth Conversions

A Roth conversion moves money from a Traditional IRA to a Roth IRA. You pay taxes now but enjoy tax-free growth and withdrawals later.

  • Best Time to Convert: When your current tax rate is lower than expected retirement rate
  • Pay Tax from Outside: Using non-IRA funds for tax maximizes the conversion benefit
  • No Income Limits: Unlike Roth contributions, there are no income limits for conversions
  • Pro-Rata Rule: If you have pre-tax and after-tax IRA funds, conversions are proportionally taxed
  • 5-Year Rule: Each conversion has its own 5-year clock for penalty-free withdrawal
